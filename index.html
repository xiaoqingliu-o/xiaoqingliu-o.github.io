About me

Welcome! I am currelty a postdoctoral research associate at Purdue University, working with Prof. Matthew Huber. I study past warm climates of the Earth using climate proxies and paleoclimate modeling. My research aims to understand how surface temperatures have responded to changes in atmospheric CO2 levels or other forcings throughout geologic history, by reconstructing sea surface temperature and atmospheric CO2 levels using organic proxies and combing them with paleoclimate modeling.
