About me

Welcome! I am currelty a senior research associate at Purdue University, working with Prof. Matthew Huber. I study past warm climates of the Earth using climate proxies and paleoclimate modeling. The central goal of my research is to advance our understanding of the evolution of ocean surface temperature patterns and atmospheric CO2 levels, how ocean surface temperatures responded to changes in CO2 concentrations and other forcings/feedback in the geologic past, and to use this information to inform projections of future climate changes.

I earned my Ph.D in oceanography from Texas A&M University, advised by Prof. Yige Zhang. I obtained my M.S. in marine geology from Tongji University under the guidance of Prof. Zhimin Jian.
